Service Type Definitions (Technical)

Residential Proxies: IP addresses assigned to real home internet users (via ISP). Traffic appears to originate from a genuine household device. Extremely difficult to detect or block. Used by fraudsters to impersonate Indian users or bypass bank/UPI security layers.

Datacenter Proxies: IPs assigned from commercial data centers (AWS, Azure, Alibaba Cloud, etc.). Faster and cheaper but more detectable. Used for bulk phishing campaigns, credential stuffing, fake account creation.

Mobile Proxies: IP addresses from real mobile carrier networks (4G/5G). Assigned dynamically by telecom towers — shared with thousands of real users simultaneously. The hardest type to block without affecting legitimate users.

VPN (ExpressVPN): Encrypts all traffic through a single server. Used to mask origin location of calls and transactions. Fraudsters combine VPN + proxy for layered anonymization.

ISP Direct Proxies (NetNut): One-hop connectivity directly through Internet Service Providers — the most trusted and hardest to detect type of IP masking available commercially.

⚠ Critical Infrastructure Risk for India

Multiple providers (711Proxy, Nstproxy, Geonode) have explicit India-based residential IP pools. This allows foreign-based fraudsters to route their internet traffic through Indian home/mobile devices, making their connection appear locally Indian to bank servers, UPI apps, and KYC systems — effectively bypassing geo-detection fraud controls entirely.

§ 05

Step-by-Step: Complete Fraud Operation Flow

How a scam targeting an Indian citizen is orchestrated from initial setup to final fund extraction — based on CBI Operation Chakra-V findings and I4C analysis

Infrastructure Setup — Scam Compound in SE Asia
Criminal operators (Chinese-directed syndicates) establish "scam factories" in Cambodia, Myanmar, Laos, or Thailand. They recruit workers (including trafficked Indians) to operate as call center agents. The compound is equipped with computers, SIM farms, and internet routing via VPN + proxy services to mask all outbound traffic as Indian.
Tech Used: ExpressVPN or similar VPN (masks country of origin) + Residential proxy (711Proxy, Nstproxy) configured to an Indian residential IP. Anti-detect browsers like Dolphin Anty or AdsPower integrated with proxy (explicitly marketed by 711Proxy)
Acquiring Indian Infrastructure — SIM Cards & Mule Accounts
Agents in India are recruited via Telegram/Facebook with offers of ₹5,000–₹20,000 per account. They provide current/savings account credentials, debit cards, and pre-activated SIM cards. These are courier-shipped or remotely handed over. The I4C identifies ~4,000 new mule accounts daily. SIM cards have been seized being smuggled to Cambodia (198 SIM cards in one seizure).
Tech Used: Telegram bots for recruitment; India-based proxy IPs used to manage accounts without triggering location alerts; UPI apps (PhonePe, Paytm, GPay) registered under mule identity
Setting Up Illegal Payment Gateways
Mule accounts are aggregated to form underground "Payment Gateway as a Service." Identified gateway brands in India include PeacePay, RTX Pay, PoccoPay, RPPay — all operated by foreign nationals. These accept deposits from fraud victims under the cover of fake investment platforms, stock trading apps, or gambling sites. Bulk payout facilities from banks are exploited to move funds rapidly.
Tech Used: Static residential proxies (NetNut, 711Proxy) to give each mule account a consistent Indian IP address; SOCKS5 proxies tunnel all traffic securely; PayGateway APIs configured with Indian bank accounts
Initial Contact with Victim — Digital Arrest / Investment Scam
Digital Arrest: Victim receives a WhatsApp/Skype call from someone posing as CBI/ED/Customs officer, claiming their phone/bank account is linked to "money laundering." The caller's video background shows a fake police station or courtroom. The call IP appears Indian (via residential proxy). Victim is placed under "virtual arrest" — told to stay on video call 24/7 and pay "bail" amounts.

Investment Scam: Victim joins a WhatsApp group offering stock tips, then directed to a fake trading app (with Indian UI, Indian proxy IP on server) where initial deposits show "profits" before victim deposits large sums — then the platform vanishes.
Tech Used: WhatsApp/Skype accounts registered behind proxies; VoIP calls routed through SIM box farms with Indian numbers; rotating residential proxies ensure each interaction looks like it originates from India; deepfake video tools layered for police-uniform appearances
Funds Collection — Into Mule Accounts
Victim is directed to transfer money to "verification accounts" or "RBI-escrow accounts" — which are actually mule bank accounts. Funds may be split across 5–20 mule accounts simultaneously. A single case (Maharashtra, Dec 2025) involved ₹58.13 crore transferred across 20,000+ mule accounts in a 40-day period using a 13-layer laundering network. Speed is critical — funds are moved within 10–30 minutes of receipt to the next layer.
Tech Used: Residential proxies allow remote control of Indian bank accounts without triggering geo-fraud alerts; automated scripts using proxy-rotating connections bulk-transfer between accounts; UPI payment layers used for speed
Layering — Obfuscating the Money Trail
Funds rapidly cascade through multiple layers: Mule Account Layer 1 → Mule Account Layer 2 → Shell Company Accounts → Crypto Exchanges → Hawala Networks. Each "hop" uses a fresh proxy IP to avoid linking transactions. Some funds are withdrawn as cash from ATMs (I4C identified 18 ATM hotspots used for cash extraction). Cryptocurrency (primarily USDT/Bitcoin) is purchased using Indian accounts and sent abroad.
Tech Used: VPN (ExpressVPN) + datacenter proxies for crypto exchange logins; mobile proxies for OTP bypass; ProxyScrape / rotating residential proxies to manage dozens of accounts simultaneously; TOR occasionally layered for additional anonymization
International Transfer — Money Exits India
Funds exit India through: (A) Cryptocurrency transfers to wallets in China/Cambodia/Dubai; (B) Hawala operators wiring funds through UAE, Hong Kong to final destinations; (C) Foreign ATM withdrawals using Indian mule debit cards — recorded in Dubai, Hong Kong, Bangkok, Russia. Once funds cross the border and convert to crypto or foreign currency, recovery becomes nearly impossible. Crypto wallets are created behind VPN to bypass KYC norms.
Tech Used: VPN (ExpressVPN, NordVPN) to mask crypto wallet creation; residential proxies to bypass exchange KYC geo-checks; BTC/USDT sent to wallets controlled by Chinese crime syndicates; Hawala networks use proxy-masked messaging apps
Funds Integration — Laundered Money Reaches Crime Bosses
Final funds arrive in accounts controlled by Chinese-linked organized crime networks operating out of Cambodia, Myanmar, or mainland China. Funds are "cleaned" through real estate purchases, shell businesses, or converted to physical gold/cash. Investigations by CBI (Operation Chakra-V) confirmed that analysis of 15,000+ IP addresses traced perpetrators to Cambodia; funds were withdrawn at overseas ATMs using Indian mule debit cards.
Tech Used: Full VPN + proxy stack used throughout; Telegram channels used for command-and-control; cryptocurrency mixers/tumblers for final obfuscation

§ 08

Recommended Countermeasures for Government Authorities

Technical, legal, and policy interventions to disrupt this fraud ecosystem

🔍
Proxy/VPN Detection at Banking Layer
Mandate RBI-regulated banks and payment aggregators to deploy real-time IP intelligence APIs (from providers like MaxMind, Spur) to flag residential proxy and VPN connections at login/transaction time. Transactions originating from known proxy ranges should trigger enhanced authentication.
🏛️
Legal Notices to Proxy Companies
Issue formal legal notices under MLAT (Mutual Legal Assistance Treaty) / Budapest Convention to proxy providers with known registration (NetNut/Israel, ProxyScrape/Belgium, Geonode/Singapore) demanding logs of Indian IP-range customers. Coordinate via I4C international desk and CBI Interpol unit.
🚫
Block Proxy Provider Domains at ISP Level
DoT/TRAI to issue directions to Jio, Airtel, BSNL, Vi to block access to proxy provider control panels (711proxy.com, nstproxy.com etc.) from Indian residential ISP connections. This disrupts domestic-based mule operators from remotely receiving proxy configurations.
🤝
International Cooperation — MoU Expansion
Expand I4C's existing MoU with US DHS (signed Jan 2025) to include UK, Israel, EU (to cover NetNut, ProxyScrape, Kape/ExpressVPN). Seek data preservation orders from Singapore authorities for Geonode logs. Activate INTERPOL I-24/7 channel for Cambodia/China requests.
📱
Mobile Carrier Behavioral Analysis
Require TRAI-compliant telecom carriers to flag SIM cards that (a) activate multiple VoIP or forwarding services, (b) are registered to addresses not matching usage geozone, or (c) receive more than 50 outbound VOIP calls/day — classic indicators of SIM-box fraud used alongside proxies.
🔐
UPI / Fintech Device Binding
RBI/NPCI should enforce device-fingerprint binding for UPI transactions above ₹50,000 — preventing proxy-masked remote account control. Implement behavioral biometrics (typing cadence, touch dynamics) to detect automated proxy-driven account operations.
📊
Mule Account Early Detection (AI)
Banks should implement ML models trained on mule account behavioral patterns: rapid small deposits followed by bulk transfers, accounts active only during 2–6 AM IST (SE Asia business hours), and new accounts receiving large international remittances within 30 days of opening.
📢
Public Awareness — "No Digital Arrest"
Continue and scale the MHA "CyberDost" awareness campaign. Key message: No legitimate law enforcement agency (CBI, ED, Police) will ever "digitally arrest" a citizen. Any such claim is a scam. Report immediately to Helpline 1930 or cybercrime.gov.in. Prime Minister Modi has already publicly addressed this issue.
⚖️
Prosecute Proxy Resellers in India
Domestic agents who resell foreign proxy services to known fraudsters can be prosecuted under IT Act Section 66C (identity theft), 66D (cheating by personation), and PMLA Section 3 (money laundering). FIR registrations should specifically name proxy service misuse as a predicate offence.
🛃
SIM Card Export Control
Ministry of Communications and Customs to establish strict protocols against bulk SIM card export. Build on existing seizures (198 SIM cards to Cambodia, 135 SIM cards seized in another case). Mandate e-KYC verification for any person activating more than 2 SIM cards in 30 days.
₿
Crypto Trail Analysis
FIU-IND and ED to partner with crypto analytics firms (Chainalysis, Elliptic) to trace USDT/BTC flows from Indian mule accounts to Chinese/Cambodian wallets. VASP (Virtual Asset Service Provider) regulations under PMLA require crypto exchanges to report suspicious Indian-account-linked crypto purchases above ₹10,000.
